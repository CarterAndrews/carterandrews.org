Description

Power Line 48 is a rhythm game themed around playing as an electronic signal on a wire. Avoid firewalls and collect power nodes by tapping along with the beat.

How we built it

Power Line 48 was made as part of the Global Game Jam event. After we formulated our team we came up with an inital goal and idea of what we wanted to make. We split up the tasks neccessary to generate the framework systems of the game, prioritizing the most vital systems. Afted we created the prototype, we tested it and analyzed parts we thought were weak. We came up with solutions for those problems and polished the final issues of the project. We released the project and had other teams at the event compete to see who could get the high score. The whole process really brought us closer together as a team.

Technology

The target platform for this game was the pc with keyboard in the browser. We used Unity and C# to build the logic of the game. I programmed a systems that made all the elements of the world except the player move down the screen to give the illusion of infinite forward motion. The game uses object pooling to improve performance by putting objects that the player has passed back in front of them. The time scale of the game is edited during runtime to provide additional difficulty. The game saves the high score of the player across multiple games.
